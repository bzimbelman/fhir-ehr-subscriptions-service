# Attendee Runbook

Welcome — this guide is for anyone joining the Community Call. The call is open, recorded, and built around your questions, so reading this once is enough.

## Before the call

1. **Register** via the form linked from the per-call document (e.g., [`2026-Q3-call.md`](./2026-Q3-call.md)) or from the announcements listed in [`communication-channels.md`](./communication-channels.md). Registration gives you the join link and a calendar invite.
2. **Submit questions ahead of time.** The registration form has a free-text field. Questions submitted at least 24h before the call get priority in the Q&A segment.
3. **Check your timezone.** The call time is published in UTC. Use a converter — `time.is/UTC` is fine.
4. **Read the [Code of Conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).** It applies to the call and any associated chat / Discussions threads.

## Joining the call

- Click the meeting link from your calendar invite or the per-call document. The meeting platform (Zoom or Google Meet) is documented in [`recording-setup.md`](./recording-setup.md).
- Join 2-3 minutes early so audio / video issues don't eat the welcome segment.
- Default to **muted with video off**. The call is recorded; only the speakers and the spotlight presenter need to be on video.
- Your display name should be something a maintainer can recognize. "iPhone" is not helpful in the Q&A.

## Asking a question

There are three ways, ranked by which gets answered first:

1. **Submit via the registration form (best)** — submitted questions are read out by the facilitator and answered on air.
2. **Drop it in the meeting chat at any time** — the notetaker collects these and the facilitator surfaces them in the Q&A segment.
3. **"Raise hand" in the meeting platform** — used only for live Q&A if there's time after submitted questions.

If your question doesn't get answered live, it moves to a Discussions thread within 5 business days. You'll be tagged so you see the response.

## Sharing your screen

Only the spotlight presenter and the maintainers share screens during the call. If you're the spotlight presenter, see the [Facilitator Runbook](./facilitator-runbook.md#spotlight-prep) — you'll be granted presenter rights at the start of your segment.

## After the call

- The recording + transcript go up on YouTube within 5 business days and are linked from `docs.bzonfhir.com/community-calls` and from the per-call document.
- Action items, deferred questions, and follow-up threads are posted to GitHub Discussions (see [`communication-channels.md`](./communication-channels.md)).
- The next call's date is announced in the wrap segment and re-posted in the channels.

## Accessibility

- Auto-generated captions are on for the live call.
- The uploaded YouTube recording has a human-reviewed transcript.
- If you need any accommodation (e.g., a screen-reader-friendly slide deck), email the facilitator listed in the per-call document at least 5 business days before the call.

## Code of Conduct

The [Code of Conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) applies to attendees, presenters, and maintainers — during the call, in the chat, and in any follow-up. Report concerns via the contact in that document; reports stay confidential.
